<p><p>The Ureteral Vascular Access Device market primarily includes Central Venous Catheters (CVC) and Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICC). CVCs are typically placed in large veins for long-term medication administration and fluid management, making them essential in critical care settings. PICCs, on the other hand, are inserted into peripheral veins and can extend to central veins, offering a less invasive option with lower complication risks. Both devices are vital for effective patient management in various medical scenarios.</p></p>
