<p><p>Biomedical imaging technologies encompass various modalities used for visualizing the internal structures of the body for diagnostic and therapeutic purposes. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) utilizes magnetic fields and radio waves to produce detailed images of soft tissues. Computed Tomography (CT) combines X-ray measurements taken from different angles to create cross-sectional images. Nuclear imaging visualizes physiological processes using radioactive substances, while ultrasonic imaging uses sound waves to create images of organs. Other emerging technologies include optical imaging and photoacoustic imaging, expanding diagnostic capabilities.</p></p>
